Best Free App Blockers for iPhone in 2026: Honest Comparison

March 28, 2026 · 8 min read

You don't need to spend $100 a year just to stop scrolling Instagram.

If you've been searching for an app blocker for your iPhone, you've probably noticed something frustrating: most of the popular options want you to pay a premium subscription just to block a few apps. Opal costs $100/year. Freedom runs $40/year. That's a lot of money to solve a problem your phone basically created.

The good news? There are genuinely free options that work. We tested every major app blocker available on iOS in 2026 and ranked them based on what matters: how well they actually help you put your phone down — without draining your wallet.

What to Look for in an App Blocker

Before we get into the list, here's what separates a good app blocker from a bad one.

Blocking that actually holds. Apple's built-in Screen Time is notoriously easy to bypass. A good app blocker makes it harder to cheat — whether through locked sessions, delayed unlocks, or accountability features.

Flexibility. Not everyone needs to block the same apps at the same time. The best blockers let you schedule breaks, create custom app groups, and adjust settings based on your routine.

Simplicity. If the app itself becomes another thing you need to manage, it's not helping. Setup should take minutes, not hours.

Price. This is supposed to help you, not become another subscription you forget about. Free or affordable wins.

2. ScreenZen — Best Free Alternative

Price: Free (donation-supported)  |  Platform: iOS, Android, macOS

ScreenZen is the other genuinely free option worth trying. It's donation-supported (not ad-supported), which means you get the full feature set without paying.

What stands out:

  • Multiple blocking strategies: delays before opening apps, interruptions while scrolling, usage goals, and hard blocks
  • Custom messaging — you can write your own "are you sure?" prompts
  • Smart tracking that only counts distracting apps (so Google Maps doesn't ruin your stats)
  • Schedule different settings for different days and times

ScreenZen is great if you want a lot of control. The downside is that the number of options can feel overwhelming at first — it's not as simple to set up as BreakOff.

3. One Sec — Best for the "Pause and Think" Approach

Price: Free for 1 app, ~$20/year for unlimited  |  Platform: iOS, Android, Chrome

One Sec doesn't actually block apps. Instead, it adds a breathing exercise before you can open them. The idea is that the brief pause breaks the automatic habit of opening TikTok or Instagram without thinking.

Research from the Max-Planck Institute found that One Sec reduces app usage by an average of 57%. That's impressive for something so simple.

The catch: the free version only works with one app. If you want to add more, you need the premium subscription at around $20/year. Still far cheaper than Opal or Freedom, but not truly free.

4. Forest — Best for Gamification

Price: $3.99 one-time (iOS), Free (Android)  |  Platform: iOS, Android

Forest turns focus time into a game. When you want to focus, you plant a virtual tree. If you stay off your phone, the tree grows. If you pick up your phone, it dies. Over time, you grow a whole forest. Plus, Forest partners with Trees for the Future — your virtual trees contribute to planting real trees.

Forest is charming and effective if you respond well to gamification. The limitation is that it's more of a "don't touch your phone" timer than a true app blocker. You can still access any app; you just lose your tree.

5. Apple Screen Time — The Built-In Option

Price: Free (built into iOS)

Screen Time is already on your iPhone. It can set daily time limits for specific apps and categories, schedule downtime, and show you usage reports.

The problem everyone knows about: it's laughably easy to bypass. When your time limit hits, you get a prompt — and one tap lets you extend by 15 minutes or ignore the limit entirely. If you have any willpower issues at all (which, let's be honest, is why you're reading this), Screen Time won't hold you accountable.

It's fine as a first step for awareness, but most people need something stronger.

The Expensive Options (And Why You Might Not Need Them)

Opal — $100/year. Beautiful design, deep analytics, focus scores, and a community. Its "Deep Focus" mode is genuinely unbypassable. But at $100/year, it's the most expensive app blocker on the market. Great if you want maximum data and accountability and don't mind paying for it. Read our full BreakOff vs Opal comparison.

Freedom — $40/year. Biggest advantage is cross-platform blocking across your phone, tablet, and computer simultaneously. If you work across multiple devices, that's a real selling point. But if you only need iPhone blocking, the price is hard to justify when free alternatives exist.
